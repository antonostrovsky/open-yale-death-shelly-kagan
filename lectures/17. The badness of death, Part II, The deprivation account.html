17. The badness of death, Part II: The deprivation account

The badness of death core Argument

<Argument for the badness of death>: Death is bad for me.
+
When I'm dead I don't exist.
+
Not existing is bad for me.
-
<The Existence Requirement>
-
<The Bold Existence Requirement>
+
<The Modest Existence Requirement>
+
<Intrinsic badness>
+
<Comparative badness>

Intrinsic and Comparative badnesses

<Intrinsic badness>: Non-existence is bad for me intrinsically
-
Non-existence can't be bad for me intrinsically. It can't be that non-existence is intrinsically bad and worth avoiding for its own sake. That would only make sense if non-existence was somehow, for example, painful. But when you don't exist, you have no painful experiences. There's nothing about non-existence that makes us want to avoid it.
<Comparative badness>: Non-existence is bad for me comparatively.
+
<The deprivation account>

The deprivation account

<The deprivation account>: Non-existence deprives you of the goods of life you might otherwise be getting.
<The tragedy of unborn people>: There are countless billions of people that could have existed, but were never born. It is therefore a great tragedy that they are deprived of goods of life they migth otherwise be getting.
+
<The deprivation account>
-
<The Modest Existence Requirement>

The Existence Requirements

<The Existence Requirement>: Something can be bad for you only if you exist.
-
<The deprivation account>
<The Bold Existence Requirement>: Something can be bad for you only if you exist at the very same time as the thing that's supposed to be bad for you.
-
<The core of Epicurus' objection>
<The Modest Existence Requirement>: Something can be bad for you only if you exist at some time or the other (i.e. unborn people never existed)

Epicurus' objection

<The core of Epicurus' objection>: There doesn't appear to be a time when death is bad for us.
+
<Metaphysical objection>: When something is true, there's got to be a specific time when it's true.
-
There are some things that are true that we can't put a date on.
+
Gunshot wound can take several days to lead to person's death.
+
<Epicurus>: "So death, the most terrifying of ills, is nothing to us, since so long as we exist, death is not with us; but when death comes, then we do not exist. It does not then concern either the living or the dead, since for the former it is not, and the latter are no more."
-
My death is bad for me DURING the time I'm dead. Because during that time, I'm deprived of the good things in life that I would be getting if only I were still alive.
+
<The deprivation account>
