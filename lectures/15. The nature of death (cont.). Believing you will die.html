15. The nature of death (cont.); Believing you will die

Sleep case

<Sleep case>: A person is in a deep dreamless sleep and presents no P-functions
-
<Simplistic view on PTPI>: The person is therefore dead
-
<Defence of PTPI>: Despite not P-functioning at the moment, the person is still able to P-function when they wake up! Abilities aren't always actualised. #

Coma case

<Coma case>: A person is in a coma and presents no P-functions
-
<Simplistic view on PTPI 2>: The person is therefore dead
+
<Defence of PTPI 2>: Unlike in the Sleep case, the person in a coma is unable to P-function upon request. Therefore the ability to P-function cannot be actualised.

Suspended animation case

<Suspended animation case>: A person is in a state of suspended animation, where he is fully intact and healthy, but is in a cryogenic sleep of some sort, but presents no P-functions at the moment
-
<Simplistic view on PTPI 3>: The person is therefore dead
-
<Argument from Ability to P-Function>: In this experiment the person simply needs to be taken out of cryogenic sleep to continue P-functioning, so the ability to P-function was never lost. The person is alive
-
<Shelly Kagan>: It is not convincing to say that a person in a state of suspended animation is alive. We need another feature to help us distinguish who is alive and who isn't in PTPI paradigm.

Believing you will die

<A1>: It is impossible to believe in yourself being dead
+
<P1>: It's impossible to imagine yourself being dead
-
It is equally impossible to imagine yourself being in a deep dreamless sleep but we still believe in dreamless sleep!
-
You can picture it from the outside, but not from the inside!

First interpretation

<Freud>: After all, one's own death is beyond imagining, and whenever we try to imagine it we can see that we really survive as spectators.
-
That is a horrible argument. We can easily imagine a meeting taking place without us, even if we "smuggle ourselves in as an observer"

Second interpretaion

<Second interpretation>: Nobody believes that they are going to undergo the death of the body
+
<Tolstoy's Death of Ivan Ilyich>: Behaviour of Ivan Ilyich indicates that he was genuinely surprised to discover that he is mortal
