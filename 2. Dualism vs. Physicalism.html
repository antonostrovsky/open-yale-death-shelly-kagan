Dualism vs Physicalism

<Question>: What does it mean that somebody has died?
<Phrasing 1>: Is there life after death?
-
<Syntax argument>: Death is the termination of life (when death comes there is no life). Therefore (at least syntactically) there can't be life after death
<Phrasing 2>: May I survive my death?
-
<Syntax argument 2>: To survive is not to die. Death implies dying. Therefore you can't survive death.
<Phrasing 3>: Will I survive death of my body?
+
<Syntax argument 3>: This is syntactically correct so we can start the discussion.

Dualist argument

<Dualist argument>: A person will survive death of his body
+
<Dualist view>: A person is Body and Soul. After the death of the Body, Soul remains.
-
<Logical flaw>: If Person is to survive death of Body, the person must be JUST the soul
-
<Mortality of soul>: Soul may not survive death of the body (even if it is separate).
+
<Pin prick argument>: Pain inflicted upon the body may influence the soul. Sufficiently heavy influence on the soul may cause its death.
-
<Phisicalist (monoist) view>: A person is a Body (that can do various things). When Body dies - a person dies too
